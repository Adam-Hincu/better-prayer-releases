## Why Better Prayer Exists

It is easy to disappear into your computer. You open your PC to work, study, browse, or play. A prayer time arrives. You think, "I'll pray in a few minutes." The minutes stretch. The computer keeps pulling you back.

Better Prayer helps Muslims break that loop. It gives you prayer times, reminders, Quran reflection, fasting times, and Islamic events in a clean interface. For users who want a stronger nudge, it includes an optional lockdown feature that locks the computer at prayer time and encourages you to get up for wudu.

The goal is not to make prayer complicated. The goal is to make it harder for your computer to quietly steal the time you intended to give to Allah.

## Who Better Prayer Is For

Muslims who want their computer to support prayer instead of distracting from it — whether you need prayer times and reminders, or an optional lockdown when salah is hardest to keep at the desk.

Better Prayer is not meant to replace intention or sincere worship. It reduces one modern problem: losing track of prayer because the computer keeps your attention.

## Prayer Lockdown

Prayer Lockdown is the flagship feature. It is designed for Muslims who use their computer heavily and want a real barrier between "prayer time has arrived" and "I will do it later."

When enabled, Better Prayer temporarily locks your device at selected prayer times. Instead of a notification you can ignore, the app creates a stronger interruption.

**Prayer Lockdown is optional.** Turn it off anytime and use Better Prayer as a normal prayer app.

### How Prayer Lockdown Works

1. A selected prayer time arrives and Better Prayer locks the device.
2. The lock screen shows a QR code and local link — connect your phone to the verification page.
3. Get up and take a picture of the sink where you make wudu.
4. A local AI model confirms you are at the sink, meaning you left the computer to do wudu — not just to unlock the screen.
5. If verified, the device unlocks per your settings. Without verification, it auto-unlocks after 15 minutes by default.

### Optional 5-Minute Enforced Lock

After successful verification, an optional setting keeps the device locked 5 more minutes — encouraging you to finish wudu and pray rather than rush back.

### Select Which Prayers Use Lockdown

Enable lockdown for any combination of Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, and Isha.
